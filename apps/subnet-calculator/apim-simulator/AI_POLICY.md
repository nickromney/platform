# AI Usage Policy

This repository allows AI-assisted work, but only when there is a real human
driver who can own the result.

## Rules

1. Disclose all meaningful AI usage in pull requests, issues, and design notes.
   Name the tool and say what it helped with.
2. The human submitting the work must understand it fully. If you cannot
   explain what changed, why it changed, and the likely operational impact
   without leaning on the AI tool, do not submit it.
3. AI can help draft text, but a human must review and edit it before posting.
   Keep submissions concise and specific to this repository.
4. Low-effort AI output will be closed. That includes generic plans, invented
   commands, shallow drive-by pull requests, or code that clearly was not run
   or understood.
5. Do not submit AI-generated media for this repository. Text and code are
   fine under the rules above. Images, audio, and video are not.

## There Are Humans Here

This repository is maintained by a human in limited time. Every issue, pull
request, and comment creates review work. Treat that boundary with respect.

AI can accelerate research, writing, and coding. It can also manufacture a lot
of plausible nonsense very quickly. The problem is not that the tools exist.
The problem is when they are used to push validation and thinking onto the
maintainer.

## AI Is Welcome Here

This is not an anti-AI policy. AI is useful and is part of modern engineering
work. The bar is simply that the human contributor remains accountable for the
result.

If you use AI well, disclose it and bring finished work. If you use AI badly,
expect the contribution to be closed.

## Maintainers

Maintainers may use AI tools at their discretion. This policy is primarily
about outside contributions and keeping review load under control.
